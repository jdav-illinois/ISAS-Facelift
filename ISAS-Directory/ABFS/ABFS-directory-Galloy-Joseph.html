General Interest/Area of Focus

Joseph Galloy is an archaeologist with primary research interests in the prehistory of the American Bottom region of southwestern Illinois. He currently serves as the Research Coordinator for the American Bottom Field Station in Fairview Heights, Illinois. Galloy’s background includes 21 years of full-time cultural resource management experience, including 15 years at ISAS and 6 years at corporate CRM programs. He completed his PhD at Harvard, where he studied Mesoamerican and North American archaeology. His dissertation examined settlement dynamics and social interaction among tribal societies in the American Bottom uplands from A.D. 650 to 900.

Current Research

Eastern Woodlands; American Bottom archaeology; African-American archaeology, especially nineteenth century Black towns; Late Woodland settlement dynamics, social interaction, feature use, and ceramic technology; North American prehistoric dogs and their variable and changing relationships with humans.

ISAS chapters and reports now in preparation include the history of investigations of the East St. Louis Mound Complex (11S706), focusing on the Mississippi River Bridge Project, the Late Woodland occupation of the Sprague site (11MO716), and the Late Woodland and Mississippian occupations at the Booker T. Washington site (11S19).
